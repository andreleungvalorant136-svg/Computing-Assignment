Problem Statement

What are the most influential car features that affect the CO2 emission the most?

Justification

This question is being researched to help inform consumers when purchasing a car, as their choices can significantly impact the environment. Many features, such as engine size, fuel type, fuel consumption, and number of cylinders, affect CO2 emissions. Our research will compare many factors to help new buyers make more environmentally informed decisions. As climate change worsens, we, as a community, need to focus on reducing our emissions. This pursuit can help ensure a greener environment and serve as a strong incentive for manufacturers to produce cleaner, more fuel-efficient vehicles.

Data Used

Dataset 1: Kaggle Car CO2 Emission

This dataset comes from Kaggle, and its author primarily derived their data from official Canadian records. The data were collected over 7 years (2014-2020) and generally describe CO2 emissions across different car features. This data includes the manufacturer, model, class, engine size, cylinders, transmission, fuel type, and fuel consumption (city and highway), allowing us to explore which factors impact CO2 emissions the most. This will primarily be used to analyze trends in CO2 emissions across manufacturers and companies. This dataset will also serve as a reference point for the other datasets used in this report, as it is arguably the most reliable, with all data coming directly from the Canadian government.

Dataset 2: Kaggle Vehicle Emission Dataset

This dataset is also from Kaggle and primarily focuses on how different conditions, such as car age, affect other types of cars and how this can contribute to overall emissions. Furthermore, it lists the vehicle's specifications, including the engine size, the type of fuel the engine runs on, its max speed and acceleration, and the company that produced the car. This dataset will be used to explore other factors that can explain how vehicle emissions differ across cars, based on their ages and usage. The dataset could also be used to check whether CO2 emissions from high-mileage cars increase with engine aging.

Dataset 3: Climate Trace

This dataset was sourced from Climate Trace, an independent greenhouse gas emissions-tracking company. They track emissions from various sources, and this dataset was pulled from the transportation section and cleaned. As described previously, the variety of sources meant the original dataset was cleaned and filtered to retain only cars, and the resulting dataset primarily includes engine and emission stats. This data is mainly useful because it provides in-depth information on the cars' specifications, as well as emissions per Litre of fuel.
